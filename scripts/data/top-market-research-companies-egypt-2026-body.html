Why Egypt Matters for Market Research in 2026

Egypt is the largest market in MENA by population (109M+) and has the strongest local pharmaceutical manufacturing base in the region, with approximately 90% of medicines consumed produced domestically. The pharmaceutical market is valued at approximately $5.8 billion and growing at 9–10% year-over-year (Fitch Solutions / BMI, 2025), making it the second-largest pharma market in Africa after South Africa.

The Egyptian Drug Authority (EDA), established in 2020, is modernising pharmaceutical regulation — streamlining product registration, pharmacovigilance, and GMP inspections. The Universal Health Insurance Authority (UHIA) and the Hayah Karima programme are expanding coverage to all governorates, driving new demand for health technology assessment (HTA), pharmacoeconomic evidence, and formulary-level data to support reimbursement decisions. For multinational pharmaceutical companies, Egypt offers scale, a growing addressable market, and an export gateway to Africa.

For market research buyers, Egypt presents specific challenges: diverse stakeholder landscapes across government and private channels, Arabic-language requirements, EDA-specific regulatory context, UHIA reimbursement pathways, and the need for culturally grounded fieldwork across Cairo, Alexandria, and Upper Egypt. Choosing the right research partner is critical.

How to Evaluate a Market Research Partner for Egypt

When shortlisting market research companies for Egypt, consider these criteria beyond standard RFP evaluation:

  • Egypt pharma project experience — track record of completed pharmaceutical and healthcare studies in Egypt
  • EDA regulatory awareness — understanding of EDA registration, pricing, and pharmacovigilance requirements
  • UHIA and payer landscape — familiarity with universal health insurance formulary processes and HTA evidence needs
  • Arabic-English bilingual execution — ability to design and field studies in both languages with medical precision
  • Physician & KOL recruitment — access to government and private hospital physicians, department heads, and KOLs
  • Quant + qual integration — mixed-method capability combining surveys, interviews, and synthesis
  • Data integrity controls — recruitment verification, response consistency, and audit trails

Healthcare vs Consumer: Choosing the Right Research Model

Egypt market research spans two broad domains: healthcare/pharma and consumer/FMCG. Most global networks (Kantar, Ipsos, NielsenIQ) serve both but through separate divisions with varying depth in pharma-specific work.

For pharmaceutical market research, the key differentiator is access to healthcare professionals. Physician surveys, KOL mapping, payer interviews, and formulary research require specialist recruitment networks and medical terminology expertise. Consumer panels cannot substitute for targeted HCP research.
