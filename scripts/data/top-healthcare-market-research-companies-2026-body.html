How to evaluate and choose a market research company

Procurement teams often default to brand recognition or panel speed. For pharmaceutical programmes, use a structured scorecard:

  1. Therapeutic and access expertise — Has the firm executed studies in your indication, care setting (community vs hospital), and payer type?
  2. Regional fieldwork depth — Can they recruit representative HCPs and payers in each target country, including GCC, EU5, UK, and emerging markets — not only via translated questionnaires?
  3. Methodology & compliance — Documented sampling plans, GDPR and local ethics alignment, ESOMAR-style governance, and audit trails for sensitive stakeholder outreach.
  4. Deliverable quality — Executive synthesis tied to one commercial decision per wave; not slide decks without implications.
  5. Speed vs rigor trade-off — Panel vendors may field surveys in days; hospital access, payer interviews, and rare-disease specialists require longer, specialist-led recruitment.
  6. Integration with syndicated data — Can the partner juxtapose primary findings with IQVIA, claims, or internal forecasts without over-claiming causality?

Request anonymized deliverable structures, sample governance notes, and references in your geography before awarding multi-country work. Many teams pair a global network for tracking with a specialist market research company for in-market execution — particularly in MENA and complex EU markets.

Services healthcare market research companies provide

Leading healthcare market research companies combine strategic and tactical modules across the product lifecycle:

Launch and market access

Physician and payer intelligence

Hospital and medtech research

Hospital formulary research, procurement interviews, and consumption audits support specialty pharma and medical device launches. Medtech programmes often require OR, cath lab, or ICU stakeholder maps alongside economic stakeholders — work that consumer agencies rarely structure correctly.
