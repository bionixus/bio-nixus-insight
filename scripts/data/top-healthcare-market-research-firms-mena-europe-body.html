Quantitative research methodologies

Quantitative healthcare research uses structured surveys, statistical analysis, and large-scale data collection to measure market size, forecast demand, track prescribing trends, and quantify competitive dynamics. Prescription data analysis complements primary surveys with objective behavioral data. Segmentation divides markets into distinct groups; conjoint analysis quantifies trade-offs between efficacy, safety, and convenience to guide development and positioning.

Qualitative research approaches

Qualitative methodologies include physician interviews, key opinion leader consultations, payer discussions, and patient focus groups to uncover decision-making processes, treatment preferences, and adoption barriers. Advisory boards convene influential stakeholders for expert perspectives on clinical practice and market trends. Ethnographic research observes professionals in natural settings to understand workflows and usability.
