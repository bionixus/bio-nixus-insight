Pharmaceutical market

The six-country GCC pharmaceutical market was valued at roughly USD 23.7 billion in 2024, growing at a 7.6% CAGR toward approximately USD 37 billion by 2030 and close to USD 49 billion by 2033. Saudi Arabia accounts for around 46% of regional spend.

Medical devices & IVD market

The GCC medical devices and in vitro diagnostic market is estimated at USD 5.5–6.2 billion in 2026, projected to exceed USD 9 billion by 2030 at approximately 8.5% CAGR. Saudi Arabia leads with ~52% regional share, followed by the UAE (~28%).

Growth across both markets is driven by Vision 2030-era hospital capacity expansion, rising chronic disease burden (diabetes, cardiovascular, oncology), and growing private-sector healthcare participation.

Saudi Arabia is the largest healthcare and pharmaceutical market in the GCC. Vision 2030's SAR 500 billion healthcare investment program is delivering new hospital capacity, private-sector participation, and insurance-funded consumption. The hospital channel accounts for approximately 42% of pharma spend through NUPCO centralized procurement.

Medical devices: Saudi Arabia's medtech market is estimated at $2.8–3.1 billion (2026), roughly 52% of GCC regional share, growing at approximately 8.3% CAGR — supported by a hospital network spanning MOH, NGHA, SANG, and KFSH&RC.

Key regulators

The UAE healthcare market reached approximately USD 22 billion in 2024, with projections toward USD 26.8 billion by 2030. Three regulatory bodies govern healthcare across the Emirates: MOHAP (federal), DHA (Dubai), and DOH (Abu Dhabi) — each with distinct requirements for market research, registration, and clinical engagement.

Medical devices: the UAE medtech market is estimated at $1.5–1.8 billion (2026), around 28% of GCC regional share, growing at approximately 9.1% CAGR — the fastest of the major GCC markets.
