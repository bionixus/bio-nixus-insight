| Fact | Source | Date |

| HTA Regulation (EU) **2021/2282** applies from **12 January 2025** | [EC HTA JCA page](https://health.ec.europa.eu/health-technology-assessment/implementation-regulation-health-technology-assessment/joint-clinical-assessments_en), [EUR-Lex](https://eur-lex.europa.eu/legal-content/EN/ALL/?uri=CELEX%3A32021R2282) | 2025 |

| **Orphan** medicines in JCA scope from **13 January 2028** | EC Q&A, Hogan Lovells / Taylor Wessing summaries | 2025–2026 |
| **All new centrally authorised** medicines from **13 January 2030** | Regulation Art. 31, EC Q&A | 2025 |
| **High-risk medical devices** JCA rules finalized; device JCAs from **2026** | Implementing Reg. (EU) 2025/2086 (Oct 2025) | 2025–2026 |
| **Joint Scientific Consultation (JSC)** is separate pre-submission pathway | Implementing Regs 2024/26, 2025/117 | 2024–2025 |
| **National pricing/reimbursement** remains member-state (AMNOG, HAS, etc.) | G-BA, Hogan Lovells | 2025 |

### Corrections for Copywriter

- ❌ Do **not** write that all new EU medicines undergo JCA in 2026.
- ✅ Use phased timeline table above.
- ✅ Distinguish **JCA** (clinical evidence synthesis) from **national HTA** (added benefit, price).

### RWE / evidence angle

- JCA focuses on **relative clinical effects** from MA dossier; RWE may appear in dossiers but is not the headline of JCA v1 scope—keep RWE discussion in “country-level divergence” section.
