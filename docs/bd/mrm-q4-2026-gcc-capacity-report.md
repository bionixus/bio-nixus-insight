## 1. Executive summary

- **Go/no-go:** **Conditional GO** — BioNixus can support the Q4 2026 GCC BD pipeline targets ($700K proposal-stage, 12 qualified opportunities, $120K–$180K bookings) **without net-new permanent headcount**, provided BD quotes land-and-expand packages first and caps concurrent multi-country scopes at **two active programmes**.
- **Capacity headroom:** ~**15.4 GCC-addressable FTE-months** in Q4 2026 (Oct–Dec) at 75% utilisation; projected GCC delivery load **13.1–16.8 FTE-months** under base-case conversion — **0–3.4 FTE-month gap** at the stress bound.
- **Primary constraint:** Multi-country launch readiness and HEOR programmes (3.0–4.5 FTE-months each, 12–16 weeks) — not single-country Access Snapshots.
- **If pipeline over-converts:** Pre-approved **1× contract Senior Research Manager (GCC)** ($8K–$12K/month, 3-month initial) closes the gap within ~4 weeks lead time; permanent hire only if Q1 2027 backlog exceeds 18 FTE-months/quarter.
- **Quoting rule (effective immediately):** BD may commit standard timelines on **Access Snapshot, Launch Diagnostic, KOL Heatmap, and Fieldwork-Only** packages; multi-country scopes **>$95K** require MRM sign-off on phased delivery before proposal send.

## 2. Methodology

### 2.1 Approach

Bottom-up **FTE-month capacity model** triangulated against:

1. **Demand side** — Q4 KPIs and revenue forecast from [BIO-43 plan](/BIO/issues/BIO-43#document-plan) §6.3, §9; tier-1 account BANT ranges from [BIO-72 enrichment](bio-72/tier1-gcc-account-enrichment.csv).
2. **Supply side** — GCC delivery pod sizing inferred from BioNixus public operating footprint (KSA, UAE, Kuwait, Egypt offices; 127+ projects / 48 clients per brand book v3, Jun 2026) and standard pharma MR project staffing benchmarks.
3. **Conversion logic** — 30% win rate (Q4 plan target), 70-day sales cycle, land-and-expand mix per plan §4.

**Domain lenses applied:** TAM/SAM/SOM (GCC serviceable slice only), MECE segmentation (land vs expand packages), triangulation (plan KPIs + account BANT + pipeline report), recency bias check (all inputs ≤30 days old).

### 2.2 FTE-month loading assumptions (per package)

| Package (BIO-43 §4) | Duration | FTE-months* | Concurrent slot weight |
|---------------------|----------|-------------|------------------------|
| GCC Access Snapshot | 6–8 wks | 1.2 | 1.0 |

## 6. Gap analysis & remediation

| Gap | Trigger | Recommendation | Cost band | Timeline |
|-----|---------|--------------|-----------|----------|
| Expand slot overflow (>2 concurrent) | 3rd multi-country or HEOR win in Q4 | **Contract SRM (GCC)** — 3-month initial | $8K–$12K/mo | 3–4 wk recruit |
| Field throughput (qual-heavy) | >40 payer interviews/month across projects | **Contract moderators** (KSA/UAE) | $3K–$5K/mo | 2 wk |
| PM bottleneck | >6 concurrent client-facing projects | Allocate +0.25 FTE PM from global pool | Internal reallocation | Immediate |
| Permanent hire | Q1 2027 backlog >18 FTE-m/qtr sustained | Hire 1 FTE RM (GCC) | $70K–$90K/yr loaded | 8–12 wk |

**Preferred path:** Contractor SRM before permanent hire (Porter's Five Forces — avoids fixed cost before demand persistence proven; aligns with plan risk §12 "capacity constraints on win").
