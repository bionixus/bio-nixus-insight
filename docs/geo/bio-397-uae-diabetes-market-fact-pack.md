## Executive summary (so what)

- **Update epidemiology to IDF Diabetes Atlas 11th edition (2024 data):** UAE age-standardised adult diabetes prevalence is **20.7%** (~**1.27M** adults aged 20–79), not 19% / 1.1M. Global rank is **11th**, not 4th.
- **Replace MOHAP-incompatible obesity stat:** National Health & Nutrition Survey 2024–2025 reports **22.4% adult obesity** and **12.5% elevated blood glucose** — do not use 31% obesity or conflate survey glucose with IDF diabetes prevalence without explanation.
- **Prediabetes framing:** IDF 2024 shows **17.0% IFG** and **18.2% IGT** separately (~1.3M and ~1.2M people). Avoid implying a single combined 17–19% without methodology note.
- **Ramadan fasting:** DAR Global Surveys (2020/2022) show **~85–95%** of T2D patients fast during Ramadan — page understates at 50–80%.
- **Expatriate share:** UAE does not publish nationality-disaggregated diabetes data. Use **~87% non-national share of total population** (GLMM/UN estimates) as demographic context, not “88% of diabetes patients.”
- **Market sizing:** For pharmaceutical revenue, cite **Grand View Research: USD 238.8M antidiabetics (2024)** with scope note; BioNixus **USD 380–440M metabolic market (2026)** is a broader BioNixus estimate — label explicitly.
- **Payer / OBP / formulary specifics:** Treat as **qualitative access intelligence**; revalidate against current DHA/DOH/MOHAP circulars before publication. OBP statements are **BioNixus primary-research claims**, not public payer policy.

## Methodology

| Lens | Application |
|------|-------------|
| **Primary vs secondary** | Secondary desk research from IDF, MOHAP, FCSC/GLMM, peer-reviewed Ramadan surveys, and one commercial sizing source (Grand View). No new primary fieldwork in this heartbeat. |
| **Triangulation** | Epidemiology triangulated across IDF Atlas country page, IDF member profile, and MOHAP NHNS 2024–2025 (different definitions — see limitations). |
| **Recency bias check** | Prefer IDF 11th ed. (2024/2025) over Atlas 10th (2023). MOHAP NHNS published 2025. Ramadan data from DAR 2020/2022 — still best available multi-country GCC subset. |
| **Signal vs noise** | Commercial market sizes vary widely by scope (drugs-only vs devices vs metabolic); present as range with scope labels, not a single “market size.” |

**Limitations:** IDF figures are modelled national estimates, not UAE census counts. MOHAP “elevated blood glucose” (12.5%) is a survey biomarker threshold, not equivalent to IDF diabetes case definition. Formulary listing status changes frequently; this pack does not audit live DHA Essential Drug List or Daman formularies. BioNixus service pricing ($75K–$200K) and recruitment success rates are **internal commercial benchmarks** — retain as BioNixus methodology claims, not third-party facts.
