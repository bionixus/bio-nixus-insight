## Copy-paste post (LinkedIn)

Most GCC launch decks fail at the payer table — not because the clinical story is weak, but because the economics arrive too late, too generic, or built for the wrong buyer.

After years of HEOR and payer research across Saudi Arabia, the UAE, and the wider Gulf, we see the same three gaps show up before the first committee meeting:

**1. One “GCC model” that no single payer accepts**  
Formulary logic differs between Abu Dhabi, Dubai, MOHAP federal programmes, and compact markets like Kuwait and Qatar. Committees spot a blended average immediately — and ask for emirate-level or buyer-specific scenarios.

**2. European dossiers pasted without localization**  
EU HTA packs rarely reflect Gulf inpatient capacity, pharmacist substitution rules, or the indirect costs payers track in budget ledgers. Without localized comparators and epidemiology priors, even strong RCT data loses negotiation credibility.

**3. Economics disconnected from field reality**  
Spreadsheets that never met a medical director, pharmacy lead, or procurement stakeholder tend to collapse on the first sensitivity question. Payer-grade evidence needs qualitative grounding — not just model architecture.

The fix is not more slides. It is sequencing: localize the economics, stress-test against real buyer archetypes, and align the narrative to how Gulf committees actually decide.
