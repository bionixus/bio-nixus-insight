## 1. Executive summary

Bionorica EG plans a price increase across four herbal Rx brands and needs **price elasticity evidence from Egyptian HCPs** before setting new price points. This pack defines the **qualitative layer** of a mixed-methods design: structured HCP interviews that explain *why* prescribing and substitution behaviour would shift at proposed price tiers—not just *how much* volume moves.

**Qual deliverables in field:**

- Specialty-stratified discussion guides (ENT, PED, URO)
- Moderator briefing with neutrality guardrails (no leading on “acceptable” price)
- Live thematic coding framework + post-field codebook
- Thick-description memo linking qual themes to quant elasticity curves (with DataAnalyst)

**Sample tiers (client-requested):** 100 / 200 / 300 physicians **per brand** at **USD 250 all-in per completed physician session** (honorarium, PM, transport, design, analytics, dashboard, QC).

**Board update (15 Jun):** **Sinupret Syrup** and **Bronchipret** are fielded in **one combined pediatric session** with the same PED HCP — not separate visits. Each tier still yields n=100/200/300 pediatricians **per PED brand**, but unique PED field sessions = tier n (not 2×n).

## 2. Business questions → qual research objectives

| # | Client decision | Qualitative objective | Saturation expectation |
|---|-----------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| RQ1 | How far can each brand move on price before HCPs reduce new starts? | Map **psychological price thresholds** and “walk-away” narratives by specialty | Stable by ~n=25–30 per specialty if quotas balanced |
| RQ2 | What substitutes/competitors absorb volume at higher price? | Document **switching logic** (clinical, economic, patient affordability) | Exploratory for Bronchipret (pre-launch) |
| RQ3 | Does channel (MOH vs private vs retail) change elasticity? | Capture **setting-specific** prescribing constraints | Requires quota across practice types |
| RQ4 | What patient-facing objections will reps hear post-increase? | Elicit **anticipated patient pushback** and counselling workarounds | Thematic saturation typical n=20+ per brand |
| RQ5 | How do KOL vs high-volume prescribers differ? | Compare **influence vs volume** segments within coverage list | Optional booster module at 300 tier |

**Researcher bias note:** Moderators are blinded to Bionorica’s target price points during fieldwork; client price scenarios are introduced only in structured quant modules (DataAnalyst-owned), not in warm-up qual probes.

## 3. Mixed-methods design (qual + quant)

```
Phase A — Qual depth (this pack)
  └─ Structured IDI per physician session (ENT / combined PED / URO)
  └─ PED session covers Sinupret Syrup + Bronchipret in one visit (~65–75 min)
  └─ Thematic coding → codebook → theme report (brand-segmented transcripts)

Phase B — Quant elasticity (DataAnalyst)
  └─ Gabor-Granger price ladder and/or Van Westendorp (PSM) embedded in same session
  └─ Discrete choice vignettes for competitive set (brand-specific)

Phase C — Synthesis (Qual + DataAnalyst)
  └─ Triangulation memo: where quant curves and qual themes align or diverge
  └─ Contradictions treated as findings (e.g., stated low sensitivity + high substitution intent)
```

**Session architecture by specialty:**

| Session type | Brands covered | Duration | Structure |
|--------------|----------------|----------|-----------|
| ENT | Sinupret Forte | ~50–60 min | Qual 20–25 min → Quant 20–25 min → Wrap 5 min |
| **PED (combined)** | **Sinupret Syrup + Bronchipret** | **~65–75 min** | Shared PED context 5 min → Syrup qual+quant → Bronchipret qual+quant → Wrap 5 min |
| URO | Canephron | ~50–60 min | Qual 20–25 min → Quant 20–25 min → Wrap 5 min |

**PED combined-session rationale:** Same pediatrician prescribes both respiratory brands; one visit avoids duplicate honoraria and reduces field fatigue. Transcripts are **segmented by brand** for coding; respondent ID is shared (`PED-###`) with brand suffix on quotes (`PED-012-SYR`, `PED-012-BRO`).

## 6. Discussion guide — core module (all brands)

**Moderator objectives:** Elicit prescribing behaviour, price sensitivity *drivers*, and substitution paths without anchoring to client target prices.

### 6.1 Introduction (5 min)

- Role, confidentiality, recording consent (Research Compliance gate)
- “We study prescribing decisions for respiratory/urology brands in Egypt—not to sell anything today.”

### 6.2 Practice & patient context (5 min)

1. Describe a typical week: what share of patients present with [condition]?
2. How do MOH vs private pathways change what you prescribe?
3. How often do patients ask about cost before accepting a prescription?

### 6.3 Current brand role (8 min)

4. Walk me through the **last time** you prescribed [BRAND]. What triggered that choice?
5. What outcomes do you expect vs alternatives?
6. For whom do you **not** prescribe it—and why?

**Probes (non-leading):**

- “What would make you choose something else?”
- “Tell me about a patient who stopped or refused.”
- “How do pharmacists interact with your choice?”

### 6.4 Substitution & competitive set (7 min)

7. If [BRAND] were unavailable tomorrow, what would you use instead?
8. Rank alternatives by **clinical fit** then by **patient affordability**.
9. Have payers or hospital formularies constrained this category recently?

### 6.5 Price sensitivity — qual only (5 min)

*No specific EGP amounts until quant module.*

10. In general, when a trusted brand increases price, what happens to your prescribing?
11. What signals tell you a price increase is “too much” for your patient mix?
12. How do you counsel patients who say a medicine is expensive?

### 6.6 Combined PED session flow (Sinupret Syrup + Bronchipret)

*Run once per pediatrician. Complete Syrup block before Bronchipret to avoid cross-brand anchoring.*

**Shared PED context (5 min)**  
- Typical respiratory patient mix; MOH vs private; caregiver price sensitivity patterns

**Block A — Sinupret Syrup (~30 min)**  
- Run §6.3–6.5 with `[BRAND] = Sinupret Syrup`  
- Transition: *“Now I’d like to ask about a related pediatric respiratory brand…”*

**Block B — Bronchipret (~30 min)**  
- Run §6.3–6.5 with `[BRAND] = Bronchipret`  
- Pre-launch probes (non-leading):  
  13. How do you manage pediatric cough today without Bronchipret on market?  
  14. What would a new herbal option need to demonstrate for you to adopt at premium vs parity pricing?  
  15. What launch price stories from other brands changed your behaviour?

**Cross-brand probe (PED only, 3 min)**  
16. If both Sinupret Syrup and Bronchipret increased price in the same year, which would you defend first—and why?

### 6.7 Close (3 min)

16. Anything we missed about pricing or substitution in this category?
17. Reconfirm anonymization; thank and transition to structured survey (quant).

## 8. Codebook framework (v0.1 — pre-field)

**Coding method:** Deductive codes from RQ1–RQ5 + inductive open coding on first 10 transcripts per specialty. Double-code 15% sample; κ target ≥ 0.75.

| Code | Definition | Example quote pattern (anonymized) |
|------|------------|-----------------------------------|
| `PRICE_THRESH` | Explicit or implicit maximum acceptable price narrative | “Beyond X EGP, parents push back every time.” |
| `SUBST_SWITCH` | Documented switch to named alternative | “I move to [Competitor] when…” |
| `CHANNEL_MO` | MOH-specific access/friction | “Tender list doesn’t include…” |
| `CHANNEL_PRIV` | Private insurance/cash dynamics | “Daman co-pay changes the decision.” |
| `PATIENT_AFFORD` | Patient inability to pay | “They buy half the course.” |
| `PHARM_SUBST` | Pharmacist-driven substitution | “Pharmacy swaps to generic unless I write…” |
| `BRAND_LOYAL` | Resistance to switching despite price | “I keep it for responders I trust.” |
| `PRELAUNCH_INT` | Intent for unreleased SKU (Bronchipret) | “I would trial if evidence shows…” |
| `KOL_INFL` | Peer or guideline influence on price tolerance | “Department head still prefers…” |
| `CONTRA_EVID` | Evidence contradicting price sensitivity | “Price rarely comes up in my clinic.” |

**Theme assembly:** Axial coding → theme candidates → member-check in synthesis workshop with DataAnalyst (quant overlay).

**Anonymization:** Quotes in client deliverables use `ENT-12`, `PED-07`, `URO-03` IDs; no governorate <5 cell sizes; no unique combo identifiers (gender + hospital + years).
