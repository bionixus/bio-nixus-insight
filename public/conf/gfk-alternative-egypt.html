What Happened to GfK — The NIQ Acquisition Explained

GfK Is Now Part of NIQ Group. GfK — Gesellschaft für Konsum-, Markt- und Absatzforschung — was one of the world's largest market research organisations, founded in Germany in 1934. For decades, GfK operated independently across consumer intelligence, market measurement, technology tracking, and media research in markets including Egypt and the wider MENA region.

In 2023, GfK was fully acquired by NIQ Group, the parent company of NielsenIQ. The acquisition combined two major consumer intelligence businesses under a single ownership structure. As a result, GfK no longer operates as an independent company anywhere in the world — including Egypt. GfK's brand, client relationships, products, and operations are being progressively consolidated into the NIQ Group structure under the NielsenIQ identity.

What This Means for GfK Clients in Egypt: Companies that previously commissioned research through GfK now deal with NIQ (NielsenIQ) as the successor entity. NIQ's Egypt operations are primarily focused on fast-moving consumer goods (FMCG) tracking, retail measurement, and consumer panel data. The specialist research services that GfK offered beyond FMCG — including healthcare, pharmaceutical, technology durables, and custom qualitative research — may no longer be available at the same depth through the combined NIQ entity in Egypt.

For organisations that need an independent, international market research agency in Egypt — one that is not solely focused on FMCG retail tracking — BioNixus provides a specialist alternative with a dedicated Cairo office and established research infrastructure across all Egyptian governorates.
