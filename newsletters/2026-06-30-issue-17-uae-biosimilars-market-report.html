UAE Biosimilars Market Report 2026

DOH treatment-naive mandate, EDE registration, SEHA inpatient rules & payer-driven switching

The UAE biosimilars market does not mirror Saudi Arabia’s single NUPCO tender model. Abu Dhabi’s DOH and SEHA policies now direct treatment-naïve patients toward listed biosimilars on eight core molecules, while Dubai and private insurers still gate switching through medical policy, brand-name prescribing, and clinician confidence—so uptake is policy-led in government facilities and negotiation-led everywhere else.

~$135M

BioNixus sizes the UAE biosimilars market at roughly USD 135 million in 2026, advancing toward ~USD 275 million by 2030 at ~20% CAGR—among the fastest-growing biologic segments in the Gulf.

What the report covers

Eight listed molecules. DOH’s August 2024 guideline directs treatment-naïve initiation on adalimumab, etanercept, infliximab, trastuzumab, rituximab, bevacizumab, filgrastim, and pegfilgrastim where MOHAP-registered biosimilars exist.

Dual-channel dynamics. SEHA mandates biosimilar use in Abu Dhabi government inpatient settings; Dubai and private insurers substitute through step therapy, prior authorization, and medical-policy refresh cycles.

EDE registration. Federal Decree-Law No. 38/2024 (effective January 2025) consolidates marketing authorisation under the Emirates Drug Establishment, with brand-name prescribing and no automatic pharmacy substitution for biologicals.
